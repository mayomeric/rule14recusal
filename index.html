RULE 14 RECUSALS

Your guide to what your alderman won't vote on...

Alderman in Chicago can recuse themselves from a vote under two different sections of law. The first requires any alderman who has any financial interest that is different than the general public or who expects to earn compensation from a party involved in legislation within the past year or in the future 12 months, must tell the Board of Ethics about such conflict, and it is posted on their website.

The second area is Rule 14 of the City Council, which requires disclosure of other potential conflicts of interest in addition to the ones described above. However, unlike those recusals, these recusals are only recorded in the City Clerk's Journal of Proceedings, and only accessible to those who are looking for them.

For the first time, we have gathered all of the Rule 14 Recusals since 2018 in one database. Explore which alderman are recusing themselves, on what bills they are recusing themselves, and the subjects of those bills.
